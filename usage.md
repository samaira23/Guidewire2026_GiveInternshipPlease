# SafetyNet App - Usage Guide

This document outlines how to run, use, and test the SafetyNet Full-stack application.

## Overview
SafetyNet is an AI-powered insurance platform covering gig workers against real-world disruptions (like rain or traffic).
- **Backend:** Django Rest Framework (DRF) running with `uv`
- **Frontend:** React + TailwindCSS

## How to Test the App

### 1. Login & Verification
- **Access the App:** Open your browser and go to `http://localhost:3000` (or `3001` depending on your active port).
- **Simulate Login:** Enter **9999999999** in the number field, select the **Worker** role, and click "Send OTP".
- **OTP Input:** On the resulting screen, you will see a convenient `Demo OTP: XXXX` generated by the backend API. Type those four digits exactly to authenticate.

### 2. Testing the Dashboard
- **Profile:** Verify that the "Worker Profile" correctly loads mock details from the database.
- **Activity & Alerts:** Wait for approx 4 seconds—you'll see the "Live Alerts" feed load random notifications from the server, reacting in real-time.
- **Risk Simulation Check:** Click the blue **"Simulate Rain Disruption"** button to check the automated redirect workflow to the Claims screen.

### 3. Testing Claims 
- **View Past Claims:** Head to the Claims tab via the sidebar. Check that past seeded claims populate perfectly at the bottom.
- **Submit a Live Claim:** Click "Submit Claim" on the Auto-Generated claim card inside the tab. Observe the status change progressively in real-time (`Submitted` ➔ `Processing` ➔ `Approved` ➔ `Paid`).

### 4. Admin & Premium Modules
- **Premium Pricing:** In the Premium Console tab, toggle the checkboxes ("Rain Risk", "Overtime Work"). It triggers an instant recalculation of premium amounts hitting the `/api/premium/calculate/` backend endpoint.
- **Admin Dashboard:** Log out by refreshing locally, sign in again but select the **Admin** role initially. Try checking out the generated charts and Risk Heatmaps.
